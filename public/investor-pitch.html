The Opportunity

Strategy

We acquire undervalued single-family residential properties in Fort Worth, TX. Each property receives strategic repairs, is leased to qualified tenants, and held for approximately 5 years. Properties are selected for strong rent yield and appreciation potential.

All operations — tenant screening, leasing, maintenance, rent collection — are managed by a licensed local property management company at 8–9% of gross rent.

Capital Structure: 2-Fund Model

We raise capital through two distinct investment funds, each with different minimum commitments:

  • Fund A: 4 equity partners × $50,000 each = $200,000 per property
  • Fund B: 2 equity partners × $100,000 each = $200,000 per property

Each fund operates independently. Partners within a fund split equity pro-rata. All equity partners share in rent income (after operating expenses) and appreciation.

Financing Approach

Properties are purchased with 30% cash down (from equity capital) and financed with a private lender mortgage at current market rates plus 1% (e.g., ~7.2% as of April 2026). This is simpler than complex deal structures — all investors are equity partners with shared upside and shared downside risk.

Key Principles

  • All equity, no fixed returns: Every investor participates in upside (appreciation + rent) and shares downside risk pro-rata.
  • Transparent cash flow: Rental income flows to property LLC; operating expenses, mortgage payments, and distributions are made according to the Operating Agreement.
  • Simplified governance: Major decisions (sale, large capital improvements, refinancing) require unanimous or supermajority consent per agreement.
  • Single exit decision: At or near the 5-year mark, partners vote to either hold, refinance, or sell. Sale proceeds are distributed pro-rata after mortgage payoff.

Financing Terms

Loan Structure

Each property is financed with a private lender providing 70% LTV (loan-to-value) over 30 years.

Down payment30% of purchase price (covered by equity capital)
Loan amount70% of purchase price
Interest rateCurrent 30-year market rate + 1.0% (e.g., 7.2% in April 2026)
Term30 years, fully amortizing
PrepaymentNo penalty; optional early payoff or refinance

Debt Service

Monthly loan payments are an LLC operating expense. Rental income must cover debt service, operating expenses (property management ~8–9% of rent, insurance, taxes, maintenance reserves), and HOA fees (if applicable). Net cash flow, if positive, distributes to equity partners pro-rata. If rental income falls short (negative cash flow), equity partners may be called for additional capital per the Operating Agreement.

Refinancing

After stabilization (typically year 2–3), the property may be refinanced if market rates improve or property value increases, reducing equity partners' capital at risk. Refinance proceeds are distributed pro-rata to partners, or reinvested per agreement.

Appendix

Legal Compliance & Risk Disclosure

Securities Law

This offering is made under Regulation D (Rule 506) of the Securities Act of 1933, with California state-level compliance under California Corporations Code § 25102. All investors must be accredited (net worth >$1M excluding primary residence, or income >$200K individually / $300K jointly).

Investment Risks

  • Real estate market risk: Property values may decline due to economic downturns, local market conditions, or unexpected structural issues.
  • Liquidity risk: Selling a property typically requires 3–6 months; emergency sales may result in below-market proceeds.
  • Tenant/operational risk: Tenant turnover, vacancy, rent default, or unforeseen maintenance can reduce cash flow.
  • Interest rate risk: While the mortgage is fixed-rate, refinancing in a higher-rate environment could be costly.
  • Leverage risk: 70% LTV means a 30% decline in property value wipes out all equity; partners may face capital calls to cover shortfalls.
  • Regulatory risk: Changes to rental laws, tax codes, or local zoning could affect property value or cash flow.

No Guarantees

Past performance is not indicative of future results. Rent and appreciation projections are estimates only. There is no guarantee of positive cash flow, property appreciation, or return of principal. Investors may lose their entire investment.

Tax Implications

This is a pass-through investment. Income, depreciation, and losses flow through to partners' personal tax returns. Investors should consult a tax advisor regarding their individual tax situation, including depreciation recapture upon sale.
