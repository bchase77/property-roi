The Opportunity

What We Do

We acquire single-family residential properties in select U.S. markets and hold them for approximately 5 years. Properties are screened for rent yield, condition, and appreciation potential. The Deal Organizer makes the final acquisition decision.

All day-to-day operations — tenant screening, leasing, rent collection, maintenance — are handled by a licensed local property management company (8–9% of gross rent, a LLC operating expense).

Why This Structure

  • Fixed-return investors (debt role) earn bank rate +1%/yr regardless of appreciation
  • Equity investors participate fully in appreciation and rental upside
  • Organizer bonus only if profitable — if investors don't do well, neither does the Organizer
  • One LLC per property — clean liability isolation, pass-through taxation
Bank loan alternative: If all investors are willing to co-sign a conventional mortgage together, the Debt Investor role disappears entirely — everyone becomes an equity partner sharing the down payment, and the bank provides the leverage at its standard rate (~6.2%). This is simpler on paper (2 roles instead of 3, no private lending terms to negotiate) but requires bank qualification for all co-borrowers, joint personal liability, and introduces foreclosure risk if rent income falls short. The current structure avoids all three.

Legal & Entity Structure

Because investors and the Organizer are California-based, California securities law governs this offering regardless of property location. Two entities per deal:

Entity 1 — Property-State LLC Holds title. Formed in the state where the property is located; registered as a foreign LLC in California ($800/yr franchise tax).
Entity 2 — California LLC (Organizer's management entity) Provides liability separation for the Organizer; reused across all future deals without re-documenting.

Attorneys Required

  • Local real estate attorney — drafts LLC operating agreement, handles closing
  • California securities attorney — Reg D + California securities compliance. Must be engaged before any investor is approached.

Agreed Operating Terms

Capital calls (unbudgeted repairs)

Any unbudgeted capital expenditure exceeding $2,500 requires a majority vote of equity investors. All costs are shared equally — 50% each — by both equity investors. The Deal Organizer may authorize repairs up to $2,500 without a vote.

Quarterly reporting

Within 30 days of each quarter end, all investors receive: profit & loss statement, cash flow summary, distribution detail, and any material property updates.

Early exit

An investor wishing to exit before year 5 has the right to sell their position and the obligation to find a qualified, approved replacement. The exiting investor bears all transfer legal costs (~$1,500–2,500). If no replacement is found within 90 days, the group votes on an early sale.

Distressed property exit

If cumulative unbudgeted capital calls exceed 10% of the original purchase price in any rolling 24-month period, either equity investor may call a Distress Vote. A vote of one equity investor is sufficient to force a market-price sale within 180 days. No investor can block this sale once the threshold is triggered. Debt investors cannot block a distress sale but are repaid in full from proceeds before equity investors receive anything.

Example: On a $289,000 property, $28,900 in unexpected capital calls over 24 months triggers the right to force a sale.

Dispute resolution

Mediation first; binding arbitration if unresolved. Court litigation is a last resort.

The Organizer's Promote (30%)

This is the only mechanism by which the Organizer earns more than the silent equity investor.

During the hold: the Organizer and silent equity investor are identical Both receive 50% of monthly net cash flow. The Organizer earns no premium during the hold period. Additional compensation is deferred entirely to the exit — and only materializes if the deal performs.

At Sale: The 30% Promote

~7.2%
Hurdle rate per year
= debt investor rate × 5 years × total equity
30%
Organizer's promote
on profits above the hurdle only

The hurdle: Total equity invested × debt rate × 5 years. This equals what debt investors earn on their capital — a high bar. Both equity investors must first generate the equivalent of the debt rate before the promote activates.

  • Below the hurdle: 50/50 split, no bonus. Organizer and silent equity investor receive identical returns.
  • Above the hurdle: Organizer receives 30% of the excess; remaining 70% split 50/50 as normal.
  • If the deal loses money: both equity investors lose equally, 50/50. No promote.

Appendix

Legal Compliance Notes — California & Federal

This section is for reference and attorney review. It does not constitute legal advice.

Why California Law Applies

Even though the properties may be located outside California, California securities law governs this offering because the Deal Organizer and most investors are California residents. California's Corporate Securities Law of 1968 is one of the most investor-protective securities frameworks in the U.S. — more stringent in several respects than federal law. Compliance with both is required for every deal, regardless of which state the property is in.

Federal — Regulation D, Rule 506(b)

Selling LLC membership interests to investors is a securities offering under federal law regardless of how it is structured. The recommended exemption is Reg D Rule 506(b):

  • Allows up to 35 non-accredited investors plus unlimited accredited investors
  • No general solicitation or advertising permitted — investors must have a pre-existing, substantive relationship with the Organizer before being approached
  • Requires a Form D filing with the SEC within 15 days of the first investment
  • No SEC registration or approval needed — it is a notice filing only
Accredited investor definition (either of the following): Net worth exceeding $1,000,000 excluding primary residence — OR — annual income exceeding $200,000 (individual) or $300,000 (joint) in each of the last two years with reasonable expectation of the same this year. Strongly recommended to limit this offering to accredited investors only.

California — Corporate Securities Law of 1968

Recommended exemption: Section 25102(f) — Limited Offering Exemption

Available when the offering is made to 35 or fewer California residents who either have a pre-existing personal or business relationship with the Organizer, or by reason of their business or financial experience can evaluate the merits and risks of the investment.

  • No advertising or general solicitation of any kind
  • Notice filing with the California DFPI within 15 days of first sale (~$300–600 fee)
  • Must be made concurrently with Reg D 506(b) compliance

Alternative: Section 25102(n) — Accredited Investor Exemption. If every California investor is accredited, this is simpler and has fewer procedural requirements. Also requires a DFPI notice filing. Recommended if the investor pool is exclusively accredited.

California Franchise Tax — Foreign LLC Registration

A property-state LLC managed from California is considered to be "doing business in California." This requires registration as a foreign LLC with the California Secretary of State and payment of California's $800/year minimum franchise tax — regardless of where the property is located or whether the LLC earns any California-source income. This applies to each property LLC individually.

California investors will receive a K-1 and owe California income tax on their share of rental income and capital gains even if the income is generated in a state with no income tax. The property state's own tax rules will also apply and vary by location.

California Community Property

California is a community property state. If any investor is married and a California resident, their investment may be considered community property. The subscription documents should include a spousal consent and acknowledgment clause for married California investors.
